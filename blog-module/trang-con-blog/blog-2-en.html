Build a Gaming PC vs Rent Thinkmay Cloud PC in 2025 — Which One Is Better?

No more spec anxiety! Learn how to play blockbuster games like Cyberpunk 2077 and Elden Ring on your phone with the power of Thinkmay Cloud PC.

By pigetgarlic •

Build a Gaming PC vs Rent Thinkmay Cloud PC in 2025 — Which One Is Better?

A few years ago, the answer felt obvious: if you want to game “seriously,” you build your own gaming PC. But as we enter 2025, the picture has changed a lot. Hardware prices are rising due to the AI wave, while Cloud Gaming and Cloud PC services — including Thinkmay — are getting cheaper, more powerful, and better optimized for latency.

So for a gamer or everyday user in Vietnam today, which choice is actually better: building a PC or renting Thinkmay Cloud PC?

Why are PC component prices surging toward the end of 2025?

The biggest reason building a PC has become harder is the rapid growth of AI. Exploding AI demand is pulling RAM, VRAM, HBM, and other GPU/server memory into data centers. Many reports show DRAM prices rising more than 170% year-over-year due to AI demand, making memory one of the fastest-increasing costs in the semiconductor industry.

The ripple effect is that GPU makers have to consider price increases. Recent analysis suggests AMD has informed partners about plans to raise graphics card prices by at least 10% due to soaring memory costs — and this may only be the next wave of increases, not the last. In addition, some mini PC brands have publicly raised prices across product lines that include RAM and SSDs, because memory components are in short supply and prioritized for the AI/server market.

Meanwhile, Cloud Gaming/Cloud PC services are moving in the opposite direction. Large platforms like GeForce NOW let you access performance comparable to RTX 40/50 series GPUs for around $10–$20 per month, especially during 50% promotions that last for weeks. Some cost comparisons also point out that building an RTX 4080 PC can cost around £2,000, while equivalent cloud gaming costs about £10–£25 per month — with almost no upfront investment.

In short: In 2025, the cost of owning real hardware is being pushed up by AI, while cloud access is “shared” across thousands of users, so each person pays far less.

The real total cost of a self-built PC

When thinking about building a gaming PC, many people only look at the GPU, CPU, and case. But the true total cost is often much higher.

First is the upfront cost. To run AAA games smoothly at high resolution, you need a mid-to-high GPU, which is directly affected by rising memory and VRAM prices. Market analysis suggests that if VRAM prices rise similarly to DDR5, GPUs could increase by at least $100 just from memory costs alone. And that’s before counting system RAM and SSDs, which are also being pulled upward by the same AI-driven demand.

Next are the “hidden” costs. A powerful gaming PC running a few hours per day consumes much more electricity than an office laptop or thin client — especially if you use a 144Hz+ monitor. You’re also responsible for maintenance: cleaning, replacing thermal paste, swapping fans, dust control, handling unstable power, and dealing with storage failures. Each GPU/RAM upgrade is another hit to your wallet.

Finally, there’s opportunity cost. A gaming PC’s practical lifespan is often 3–5 years before it starts feeling “weak” for new games. With GPU generations evolving faster and RTX 50 series entering cloud and desktop, your build may fall behind even sooner than expected.

Thinkmay Cloud PC: from “buying assets” to “renting power”

A Cloud PC model like Thinkmay completely changes how you access hardware performance. Instead of paying a large lump sum for a PC and living with it for years, you simply rent the configuration you need weekly or monthly.

Essentially, you move from CAPEX (buying assets) to OPEX (paying for a service). There’s no huge upfront cost. You only need an endpoint device capable of streaming: an office laptop, a MacBook, or an old PC. The CPU/GPU/RAM/SSD power runs on Thinkmay’s servers.

When your gaming/workload changes, your cost changes too. During exam periods or business trips, you can pause or use less. During holidays, you can subscribe to a stronger plan to play heavier games. Many cloud vs traditional PC comparisons highlight that cloud gaming has far lower entry cost, while performance and image quality keep getting closer to physical machines.

Another advantage is that Thinkmay isn’t only for gaming. You can also use Cloud PC for heavy tasks like video rendering, 2D/3D design, and running Windows-only software on a MacBook — without upgrading your real device.

Real-world experience: latency and image quality in 2025

One of the biggest concerns about Cloud PC is latency. Early cloud gaming was labeled “laggy” and unsuitable for fast-paced games. But streaming tech and network optimization in recent years have narrowed that gap significantly.

With Thinkmay (or any Cloud PC with servers close to users), the signal path is much shorter than playing on servers in Europe or the US. With stable fiber Internet, enough bandwidth, and optimized routing, latency for most RPGs, co-op games, open-world titles, and casual MOBA play can be totally acceptable. Only in professional esports scenarios — where players demand absolute frame-perfect precision — does a physical PC still hold a clear advantage.

In other words, for the majority of casual gamers, cloud latency in 2025 is no longer a “can’t play” barrier — it’s simply a factor to consider based on game type and your home network quality.

Who should build a PC, and who should choose Thinkmay Cloud PC?

Build a PC: If you’re a hardcore player who plays competitive FPS, pushes high ranks, wants control over every frame, uses a high refresh-rate monitor, and accepts the financial risk of hardware value dropping.

Choose Cloud PC: If you’re a student or working professional using an office laptop/MacBook, with limited time for gaming. You want to avoid big upfront costs and hardware price volatility driven by AI.

Thinkmay Cloud PC is especially suitable for “work + play” users: use Cloud PC during the day for heavy software and projects, then switch to gaming at night without needing a second machine.

Conclusion: 2025 is a great time to start with Cloud PC

As PC component costs remain under pressure from the AI market, building a new gaming PC is becoming a heavier financial decision.

For most everyday users in Vietnam, a smart approach is to begin your gaming journey with Thinkmay Cloud PC. Try it for a few weeks to see the real experience. If the answer is “stable, fun, and no upgrades to worry about,” you can let Thinkmay handle the hardware — while you focus on enjoying games and getting work done.
